A former EMT, my spine decided I should stop lifting human beings. So, I decided that a complete life refactoring was in order. I left New Haven, CT, towing a travel trailer and looking for a new path. A week after deciding that Nashville was home I met a woman sitting at the bar on election night, who told me about the Nashville Software school.

While healing I had done some WordPress web design, as well as some ad design for a non-profit and a couple of restaurants. I knew enjoyed delivering a finished product to the client almost as much as I loved tinkering on my computer. However, I found my lack of coding skills limiting. As I thought about a new career I ruminated on the stranger's advice about coding school and her roommates new career as a developer. Then I applied to NSS.

I am loving every "ah ha" moment of coding from my first “Hello World”, to building and querying databases. I found that I relish planning and organization. At some point I began flow-charting and Trello-boarding; beacuse of these tools I became passionate about coding and the challenge it presents. Finally, building my capstone projects allowed me to solve real problems with the skills I am attaining; I find the tangible, clickable fruits of my labor truly rewarding.

Now, with the travel trailer stowed away, I am pursuing my development career, bringing to the table my medical background and my passion for learning, planning, and refactoring.
